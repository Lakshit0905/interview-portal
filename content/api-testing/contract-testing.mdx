## The problem it solves

End-to-end tests across many services are slow and flaky, and they catch integration breaks *late*. Contract testing verifies the **interface** between a consumer and a provider in isolation.

## Consumer-driven flow

1. The **consumer** writes expectations ("when I GET /users/1, I expect this shape") which generate a **pact** file.
2. The pact is published to a broker.
3. The **provider** replays the pact against itself in CI and must satisfy it.

Either side breaking the agreed shape fails fast, in unit-test time, with no shared environment.

## When to reach for it

- Many services owned by different teams.
- You want deploy independence with a safety net.
- You don't need full behavioral coverage at the boundary — just shape and semantics.

## Interview framing

Contrast it with schema validation (static) and full E2E (behavioral, slow). Contract testing sits in between: cheap, fast, versioned, and it pinpoints *which* side broke the agreement.
